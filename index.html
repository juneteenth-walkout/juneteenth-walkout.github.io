Juneteenth
Call to Action

In celebration of Juneteenth, and in solidarity with the Black Lives Matter protests, join your fellow Esri employees for a virtual walkout on Friday, June 19th @ 2 PM PST for 15 minutes or more.

Go to Registration

Let’s use this time off to observe Juneteenth, recognize Black history, and pledge to support racial and social justice within Esri and beyond. However, observing Juneteenth alone is inadequate. Let’s all reflect on ways that we can support and work towards improving our culture at Esri, specifically for underrepresented Black and Indigenous employees. Let’s signal beyond just our internal community that we stand with our country as one body that will do each of our parts in repairing our internal practices to be more equitable, with hopes that it will externally make an impact on the communities we serve.

How

Learn more about Juneteenth history. On Friday, June 19th, use sick time and/or PTO and stop working at 2 PM PST. Make sure to let your supervisor/manager know why you're doing it. Feel free to include this flyer in your notice.

What should I do once I've walked out

  • Read or watch a movie on Black history
  • Attend a local Juneteenth celebration
  • Register to vote, if you are eligible
  • Support or volunteer at a local Black non-profit organization
  • Support a Black Owned Business
  • Write or call local elected officials. Ask them to reevaluate the police budget and reinvest in money into Black education and social services
  • Register for the event, so we can ask Esri to make Juneteenth a paid holiday
