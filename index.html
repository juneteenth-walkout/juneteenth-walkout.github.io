Juneteenth Walkout

In celebration of Juneteenth, and in solidarity with the Black Lives Matter protests, join your fellow employees for a virtual walkout on Friday, June 19th.

Demand Esri to observe Juneteenth as a paid holiday, recognize Black history, and pledge support for racial and social justice. However, observing Juneteenth alone is inadequate. The under representation of Black and Indigenous employees at Esri signals a systemic problem that mirrors our country and as a community, we must demand Esri to assess and repair its hiring practice to be more equitable.

How

Learn more about Juneteenth history. On Friday June 19, use sick time, PTO, or just stop working at 2 PM. Make sure to let your supervisor know why you're doing it. Feel free to include this flyer in your notice.

What should I do once I've walked out

  • Read Black history. Most of it were erased or edited from history lessons.
  • Attend a local Juneteenth celebration.
  • Register to vote, if you are eligible
  • Volunteer at a local Black non-profit organization
  • Write or call local elected officials. Ask them to defund the police and invest the money to Black education and social services.

What if I can't

If you have prior plans, we ask that you voice your support of this employee action and share this flyer with colleagues.
